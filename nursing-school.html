About the School

A Tradition of Nursing Excellence

The CHS School of Nursing was established as an integral part of Christian Hospital Sialkot to meet the growing demand for qualified nursing staff in the Punjab region. Rooted in the hospital's mission of faith-based, compassionate care, the school has produced hundreds of skilled nurses who serve across Pakistan.

Students receive a blend of rigorous classroom instruction and hands-on clinical training within the live wards and departments of CHS — one of Punjab's most experienced hospitals. This real-world exposure, guided by senior nursing and medical staff, ensures graduates are fully prepared for the demands of modern healthcare.

Academic Programs

Programs We Offer

All programs are recognized by the Pakistan Nursing Council (PNC) and lead to nationally accepted nursing qualifications.

Most Popular

Diploma in General Nursing (DGN)

3 Years (6 Semesters)

The flagship program of CHT School of Nursing, this three-year diploma develops comprehensive nursing skills covering all major clinical areas. Graduates are eligible for PNC registration as Registered Nurses (RN).

  • Anatomy & Physiology
  • Fundamentals of Nursing
  • Medical-Surgical Nursing
  • Obstetric & Gynecological Nursing
  • Pediatric Nursing
  • Community Health Nursing
  • Psychiatric Nursing
  • Nutrition & Dietetics
20–25 Seats/Year Apply Now

Diploma in Midwifery (DM)

2 Years (4 Semesters)

A specialized program focused on maternal and newborn care. Graduates become qualified midwives, addressing the critical need for skilled birth attendants in rural Punjab communities.

  • Antenatal & Postnatal Care
  • Normal & Complicated Labour
  • Neonatal Nursing
  • Family Planning Counselling
  • Obstetric Emergencies
  • Safe Motherhood Practices
15–20 Seats/Year Apply Now

Post-RN Certificate Courses

6–12 Months (per course)

Continuing education courses for qualified RNs who want to specialize or upgrade their skills in specific clinical areas — available to serving nurses at CHT and external candidates.

  • Critical Care Nursing
  • Operating Theatre Techniques

  • Emergency & Trauma Nursing

Training Structure

Clinical Rotations & Training

Students complete supervised rotations across all major departments of Christian Hospital Sialkot, gaining real-world clinical competence from day one.

Obstetrics & Maternity

Labour ward, antenatal clinic, and postnatal care — CHS's specialist strength.

Pediatric Ward

Newborn and child care, vaccination clinics, and acute pediatric nursing.

Surgical Ward & OT

Pre- and post-operative care, wound management, and theatre assistance.

General Medicine

Medical ward management, medication administration, and patient monitoring.

Emergency & Casualty

Triage, first aid, emergency protocols, and trauma nursing under supervision.

Community Outreach

Rural health campaigns, vaccination drives, and community health education.

Laboratory & Diagnostics

Specimen collection, lab procedures, and understanding diagnostic results.

Mental Health Awareness

Basic psychiatric nursing, patient communication, and mental health first aid.

Campus & Infrastructure

Our Facilities

Nursing Skills Laboratory

A fully equipped skills lab with simulation mannequins, IV practice kits, wound care sets, and all clinical instruments — allowing students to practise procedures safely before entering live wards.

Library & Study Centre

A dedicated library stocked with nursing and medical textbooks, PNC exam guides, journals, and reference materials. A quiet study hall is available for students throughout the day.

Female Students' Hostel

Safe, clean, and secure on-campus hostel accommodation for female students, with separate dining, prayer room, common room, and 24-hour security — purpose-built for nursing trainees.

Classrooms & Lecture Halls

Spacious, well-lit classrooms equipped with whiteboards, projectors, and audio-visual aids. Dedicated seminar rooms support small-group teaching and case-study discussions.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum Matriculation (SSC) — Grade C or above
  • Science subjects preferred (Biology, Chemistry)
  • Age: 17–28 years at time of admission
  • Physically and mentally fit (medical examination required)
  • Female candidates only for Diploma General Nursing
  • Valid CNIC / B-Form copy
